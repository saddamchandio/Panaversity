# Reference — Internal genres

Office orders, notices, POL statements, and noting sheets. These have fixed scaffolds; fill the variables and keep the set phrases exact.

---

## 1. Office Order

**Heading:** `OFFICE ORDER` (centered, bold).

**Opening formula (pick one):**
- New posting: `Following transfer/ posting is hereby ordered with immediate effect till further orders in the best interest of company's works.`
- Supersession: `In suppression of all previous orders, following internal arrangement is being ordered with immediate effect till further orders in the best interest of company's works.`

**Body:** a table.
- Posting: `Sr# | Name of Officer | New Assignment` (add a `Remarks` column when a caveat applies, e.g. *"The officer will also assist the E&M section till the commissioning of dedicated 11KV feeder of this office."*).
- Driver/vehicle allocation: `Sr# | Name of Vehicle Driver | Vehicles Allocated for Official Duties`.

**Signature + CC.** CC includes the controlling offices, then `Officer concerned.` (or `Officials concerned. **For strict compliance, please.**`), ending `Master File.`

---

## 2. Notice

**Heading:** `NOTICE` (centered, bold).

**Shape:** address the affected group → give the reason → state the rule, often with a small timings/particulars table → advise compliance → close warmly.

**Distinctive lines:**
- `This is to inform all employees utilizing [the facility] that, due to [reasons], the [facility] will now operate at the following fixed timings:`
- Table, e.g. `Morning Departure from ISMO Residential Colony: | 08:15 AM` / `Afternoon Departure …: | 12:45 PM`.
- `Please note that the van will only make one round trip per shift. Kindly ensure your child's schedule aligns with these timings to avoid any inconvenience.`
- `We regret to inform you that, due to fuel limitations, individual adjustments … cannot be accommodated. If these timings are unsuitable, employees may choose to discontinue using the facility.`
- Close: `We appreciate your understanding and cooperation.`

**Signature:** usually Deputy Director (E&M, Transport). CC includes the relevant directors, the drivers (*For strict compliance, please.*), the affected employees (*For your kind information, please.*), `Master File.`

## 4. Noting sheet (internal, for approval up the chain)

**Header** (top of every page of the note):
```
Office of the Senior Director (SN & VRE Op.) RCC(S) Jamshoro
Under Executive Director System Operator
```

**Numbered sections** (this is the spine):
1. `Subject:  <…>`
2. `Background:` — dash bullets; **bold the load-bearing facts and dates** (e.g. *non-operational since **2010***, *repair estimate of **PKR 15,21,572/-***, *NEPRA Delegate, during their visit on **09.04.2025**, …*).
3. `Issue:` — the problem stated as consequences; bold the categories (*operational disruptions, employee inconvenience, safety & compliance risks*).
4. `Recommendation:` — the proposed action.
5. `Submitted for Approval:` — fixed close: `It is requested that competent authority may please be approached for approval of the proposed actions as requested at NP-<nn> to ensure operational efficiency.`

Where the note tabulates a request (e.g. IT equipment), use a table (`Sr No | Name | Designation | Equipment Deployed | Equipment Required`) inside the Background/Subject section, then the same `Submitted for Approval` close.

**Originating officer** signs first (e.g. `Assistant Director (E&M, Transport) / ISMO – RCC(S) Jamshoro`).

**Submission chain** — each subsequent approver adds a numbered line continuing the note's numbering:
```
6. Submitted, please.
Dy. Director (E&M, Transport) ISMO - RCC(S) Jamshoro.

7. Submitted, please.
Director (South Operations) ISMO - RCC(S) Jamshoro.

8. Submitted, please.
Senior Director (SN & VRE Operations) ISMO - RCC(S) Jamshoro.

9. Submitted, please.
Executive Director (System Operation) ISMO Islamabad.
```
`NP-<nn>` (note-page) cross-references point back to the relevant note number in the file. Keep the chain in ascending designation order toward the competent authority.
