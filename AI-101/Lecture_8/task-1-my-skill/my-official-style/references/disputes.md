# Reference — Disputes (rebuttals & point-by-point replies)

The high-stakes genres. Tone is firm and evidence-led, but the surface courtesy never drops and the letter still closes on cooperation and national interest. **Never fabricate a clause number or quoted Grid Code text** — use only what the user supplies, and bracket anything unverified as `[Clause __ — verify exact text]`.

---

## 1. Technical / Grid-Code rebuttal

**Purpose:** correct a flawed or jurisdictionally wrong position taken by another office, anchored in the NEPRA Grid Code 2023 (or PPA/BTA/rules as applicable).

**Shape:**
1. Reference the offending correspondence and name the flaw squarely.
   - `It is written with reference to the correspondence referred at Sr.No.(ii) regarding [matter]. While the commitment to [X] is noted, the advisory in your letter's copy section, suggesting that [their position]; is fundamentally flawed, legally invalid, and demonstrates a concerning unfamiliarity with the current regulatory framework.`
2. `To rectify this gross misunderstanding of the debundled market structure and the statutory mandate of the System Operator, the following explicit provisions of the NEPRA Grid Code 2023 are brought to your immediate attention:`
3. **Numbered legal arguments.** Each: bold the clause ID, state the point, then quote the clause text in *italics inside quotation marks*, then apply it to the facts.
   - `**1.** As per **Clause OC 1.2.(c)** [their suggestion] is technically illogical and operationally hazardous. Under this clause, the Grid Code explicitly dictates that: *"… [verbatim clause text] …"* [Then: how the facts engage this clause.]`
   - Sub-points may be dash-bulleted, each opening with a bold clause ID (`**Clause CM 10.1** mandates:`, `**Clause OC 6.1.3** establishes absolute authority …`).
4. **Definitional contrast** where roles are confused: quote the licensed role of the **System Operator** vs the **Transmission Network Operator (TNO)** to show the other party is conflating a wire business with constraint management.
5. **Close** — recommend they get current, then pivot to cooperation:
   - `We highly recommend that [HESCO]'s regulatory and compliance teams familiarize themselves with the NEPRA Grid Code 2023 to avoid similar administrative misinterpretations in the future. Understanding these fundamental jurisdictional boundaries will undoubtedly streamline our mutual efforts. In the best national interest we look forward to your prompt action regarding [the actual deliverable].`

**Voice:** this genre uses **"we"/"this office"** rather than "the undersigned." Bold key technical terms and the disputed phrases (e.g. the quoted advisory you are rebutting). Keep it surgical: every assertion is tied to a clause or a fact, never to indignation.

**Signature:** typically Senior Director (SN & VRE Operations); CC widely (ED-SO, CEO of counterpart, relevant CEs, NGC/CPPA-G as applicable), ending `Master File.`

## 2. Point-by-point tabular reply

**Purpose:** answer a letter that makes several claims/queries/allegations, rebutting or responding to each in a table so nothing is left unaddressed.

**Shape:**
1. **Opening paragraph** situating the reply:
   - `With reference to the above referred letter at Sr.No.(i) a correspondence has been made by [office] in response of this office letter at Sr.No.(ii). Wherein the following important points are apprised:` — or for a query list — `In the light of above referred letter, kindly find below the following query-wise response:`
2. **Two-column table.** Header pair is one of:
   - `Correspondence of [their office]` | `This office remarks`
   - `Query from your office` | `This office response`
3. **Each row:** their point verbatim/summarised on the left; the response on the right. In responses:
   - Open with the substance, cite clauses where they help (`in accordance of **NEPRA Grid Code 2023 Clause OC 4.11.4**: *"… [verbatim] …"*`).
   - Label bad claims plainly: **`This claim is misleading because …`** / `Such conclusions are factually incorrect …`.
   - Marshal the real chronology and the binding obligations on the other party (e.g. `it is obligatory under relevant PPA that N-1 contingency to be provided to these plants which is not present yet and the work remains pending on part of NGC`).
   - Where a query has no separate answer, leave the response cell blank and address it in an adjacent row (he does this when several of their points collapse into one rebuttal).
4. **Closing paragraph** — the constructive ask + a call to professionalism:
   - `… it is also requested to approach the office of [CE (P&C) NGC Lahore] to carry out the Root Cause Analysis … Moreover, the officer concerned may also be advised to maintain cordial professionalism, avoid misleading and factually inaccurate write ups in order to maintain good faith and ensure mutual cooperation in the best interest of National Grid.`
5. **Signature + wide CC.** In reminders/replies of this kind a CC entry often carries the back-reference: `Dy. Manager T/L (M) Div. NGCPL Jamshoro. **w.r.t. your office letter no. [____] dt: [__.__.____]**.`

**Tabular-reply tone:** scrupulously factual, quietly devastating. The courtesy formulas stay; the force comes from chronology, clauses, and the other side's own obligations — not from adjectives.
